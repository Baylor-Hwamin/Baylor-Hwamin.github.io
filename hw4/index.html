Korean Cuisine: Eobokjangban

Eobokjangban (eo-bok-jang-ban): A dish that contains several pieces of beef cuts in a brass tray.

It is one of the local foods in Pyongyang. It is generally known as a food that originated and developed in the shopping district of Pyongyang. For this reason, when a hostile feeling arises due to mutual interests while bargaining, it is sometimes interpreted that it develops in a mall because hostility or tension is relieved and demanding entertainment becomes easier.

To make it, boil the cow's head meat, brisket, and breast meat, slice it into thin slices, and season it. Then, put egg cracker, green onion, pear, and pine nuts as garnish and place the soy sauce in the middle and pour hot broth.
